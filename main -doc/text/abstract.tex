\begin{abstract}
Credential fraud and inefficient verification remain persistent challenges in the global labour market. Existing technological solutions operate in isolation: blockchain systems secure credentials but do not assess underlying skills, AI evaluates competencies but produces no verifiable records, and matching algorithms degrade when operating on unverified data. This research investigates whether integrating these three technologies into a unified pipeline improves the reliability of job-candidate matching. Following a Design Science Research methodology, the study designs and evaluates SkillChain, a platform in which AI-powered assessments produce blockchain-verified credentials that feed into a weighted matching algorithm. The evaluation compares matching accuracy with and without verified credential data to measure the extent of improvement.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Keywords:} Blockchain, NFT Credentials, Smart Contracts, Skill Verification, AI-Powered Assessment, Machine Learning Job Matching, ERC-721, Decentralized Identity
\end{abstract}
